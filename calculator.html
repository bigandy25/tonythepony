Why minutes, not appointments

A "small ask" is rarely small once you count every pair of hands it passes through. Asking a practice to do a blood test before a hospital appointment might mean five minutes of admin time to set a recall and call the patient in, five minutes of HCA time to take the bloods, and five minutes of GP time to review the result, check the notes, and realise it wasn't for them anyway.

Nobody wants a transactional system billing for every few minutes — but at system level these small asks add up, and they need to be counted somewhere. This tool helps put a number on the cost of an individual ask, as distinct from a whole service.
